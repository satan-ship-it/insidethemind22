The Nature of Stress

Stress is not just an external event; it is a transactional process between you and your environment. This section explores what stress actually is, distinguishing between beneficial challenges (Eustress) and harmful strain (Distress).

What is Stress?

‚ö°

It is a pattern of responses to a stimulus that disturbs equilibrium and exceeds coping abilities.

Etymology: From Latin strictus (tight) & stringere (to tighten). Reflects the internal feeling of constriction.

Cognitive Appraisal Theory

üß†

Proposed by Lazarus: Stress depends on how an event is interpreted. Click the buttons below to trace the process.

1. The Stressor Event Start
Something happens (e.g., a new project, a loud noise). The mind asks: "What is this?"
2. Primary Appraisal Does it matter?
Perception of the environment:
  • Positive/Neutral: No stress.
  • Negative: Evaluated as Harm (damage done), Threat (future damage), or Challenge (opportunity).
3. Secondary Appraisal Can I cope?
Assessing resources (mental, physical, social).
Outcome: If resources > demand, stress is low. If resources < demand, stress is high.

General Adaptation Syndrome (GAS)

Hans Selye's 3-Stage Model of prolonged stress response.

1. Alarm 2. Resistance 3. Exhaustion
Chart Interpretation: The body initially dips in resistance (shock), then rallies resources to cope (Resistance), but if stress persists, resources deplete leading to Exhaustion and disease susceptibility (burnout, high BP).

Stress Resistant Personality

People with high stress but low illness often possess Hardiness, characterized by the "3 Cs":

1. Commitment Personal commitment to work, family, hobbies.
2. Control Sense of purpose and direction in life.
3. Challenge Viewing change as normal/positive, not threatening.
