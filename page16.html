Module 1

The Anatomy of Attitudes

An attitude is a state of mind or a set of views regarding a specific topic (the 'attitude object') that has an evaluative feature.

The A-B-C Components

Affective A

The emotional component. Feelings like happiness, anger, or fear associated with the object.

Behavioural B

The conative aspect. The tendency to act toward the attitude object.

Cognitive C

The thought component. Individual knowledge, beliefs, and logic about the object.

Cognitive Dissonance

Dissonance: Holding two conflicting cognitions (e.g., smoking is bad vs. I smoke).

Resolution requires changing one cognition, usually by altering behaviour.

Two-Step Concept

1. Identification with an admired source.

2. Source changes own behavior; Target observes and imitates.

Presence of Others

Pro-social Behaviour

Voluntary action to benefit others at a personal cost. Driven by empathy and social norms.

Reciprocity

Equity

Responsibility

The Bystander Effect
