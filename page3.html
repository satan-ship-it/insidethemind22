Introduction to Psychological Enquiry

Psychology is more than treating mental illness; it's a rigorous scientific discipline for understanding human thought, feeling, and behavior. This section outlines the foundations of **psychological enquiry**, a systematic process that uses empirical research methods to study experience and mental processes.

A Scientific Approach

Psychology earns its place as a science through its use of **systematic observations**, controlled conditions, and **empirical research methods** to study behavior, experience, and mental processes.

Nature of Psychological Data

Data encompasses covert and overt behaviors, mental processes, subjective experiences, and contextual factors (physical, cultural, genetic). Crucially, data must always be **interpreted within context**.

Paradigms of Research

Scientific Tradition

Assumes behavior is observable, measurable, and predictable. Relies on controlled, empirical methods for objective conclusions.

Interpretive Tradition

Focuses on **meaning-making**. Views behavior as complex and context-bound, emphasizing subjective understanding within a social context.
